About & methodology

Who's behind this

These tools are built and maintained by Shan Naqvi, a UAE-based qualified accountant based in the UAE. After seeing how many local "mortgage calculators" either oversimplify the rules or quietly get them wrong, I built calculators that apply the actual Central Bank of the UAE regulations — and cite the source behind every figure.

How the numbers are sourced

Every rule used in these calculators is taken from a primary source and re-checked, not copied from other blogs. The key references are:

  • CBUAE Mortgage Regulations (Circular 31/2013), Article 3 — LTV caps, the 25-year tenor, the income multiple and the stress test.
  • CBUAE Resolution 31/2/2020 — the 2020 amendment that raised first-time-buyer LTV by 5%.
  • CBUAE Regulation 29/2011 — the 50% Debt Burden Ratio cap and credit-card treatment.
  • Dubai Land Department fee schedule — transfer, registration and trustee fees.

Where a figure is lender practice rather than a Central Bank rule — for example the 5% of credit-card limit, or non-resident LTV bands — it is labelled as such, with an amber marker, so you always know what is regulation and what is convention.

What these tools are (and aren't)

They are free, independent, browser-based estimators to help you understand your likely borrowing range and costs before you speak to a bank. They are not financial advice and not a loan offer. Banks apply their own credit policy, income assessment, property valuation and live rates, so your actual approval may differ. Please see the full disclaimer.

Corrections & contact

Rules change, and accuracy is the whole point of this site. If you spot something that looks out of date or wrong, please tell me at shannaqvi90@gmail.com and I'll review it against the source.
